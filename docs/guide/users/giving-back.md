# :material-gift-outline: Giving Back

Every open-source project you use was built and maintained by people who
chose to share their work. Even if you never write a line of code, there are
many ways to support the projects and community you depend on.

---

## Report Bugs Effectively

Bug reports are one of the most valuable contributions a user can make. A
good bug report saves maintainers hours of debugging.

### How to write a great bug report

!!! abstract "Bug report checklist"
    1. **Search first** — Check existing issues to avoid duplicates.
    2. **Use the template** — Many projects provide an issue template.
       Fill in every section.
    3. **Steps to reproduce** — List the exact steps to trigger the bug.
    4. **Expected vs actual behaviour** — What should happen? What happens
       instead?
    5. **Environment** — OS, browser, Power Platform version, tool version.
    6. **Screenshots or logs** — Visual evidence helps enormously.

!!! example "Good bug report"
    **Title:** Export fails with timeout when dataset exceeds 10,000 rows

    **Steps to reproduce:**
    1. Open the export dialog
    2. Select a table with more than 10,000 rows
    3. Click "Export"

    **Expected:** Export completes within 60 seconds
    **Actual:** Timeout error after 30 seconds

    **Environment:** Windows 11, Edge 120, Dataverse production, v2.3.1

## Participate in Discussions

Many projects use **GitHub Discussions** for conversations that are not bugs
or feature requests. This is where you can:

- **Ask questions** — Get help from maintainers and other users.
- **Share use cases** — Explain how you use the project. Maintainers love
  hearing real-world stories.
- **Answer others' questions** — If you have solved a problem, help the
  next person who encounters it.
- **Suggest ideas** — Float ideas before they become formal feature
  requests.

!!! tip "Be constructive"
    When requesting features, explain the **problem you are trying to solve**,
    not just the solution you imagine. Maintainers can often suggest better
    approaches.

## Star and Watch Repositories

### Starring

Clicking the :star: **Star** button on a repository:

- **Signals quality** — Stars are the primary trust indicator in open source.
  More stars help a project attract contributors and users.
- **Bookmarks it** — Your starred repos appear on your GitHub profile and
  in your personal star list.
- **Costs nothing** — It takes one second and makes a real difference to
  maintainers.

### Watching

Clicking **Watch** subscribes you to activity notifications:

- **All Activity** — Every issue, PR, and discussion.
- **Releases Only** — Get notified when new versions are published
  (recommended for tools you depend on).

## Financial Support

Many maintainers work on open-source projects in their personal time. Financial
support helps them sustain their efforts.

### GitHub Sponsors

[GitHub Sponsors](https://github.com/sponsors) allows you to fund developers
and organisations directly through GitHub. Look for the :heart: **Sponsor**
button on a repository or profile page.

### Other platforms

| Platform | How it works |
|----------|-------------|
| [Open Collective](https://opencollective.com/) | Transparent budgets for open-source projects |
| [Buy Me a Coffee](https://www.buymeacoffee.com/) | One-time or recurring support |
| [Patreon](https://www.patreon.com/) | Membership-based support tiers |

!!! tip "Corporate sponsorship"
    If your organisation depends on open-source tools, consider establishing
    a budget for sponsoring the projects you use. Even small amounts send a
    powerful signal that the work is valued.

## Advocacy

Word of mouth is powerful in the Power Platform community.

### Ways to advocate

- **Share on social media** — Tweet, post on LinkedIn, or write a blog post
  about a tool you found useful.
- **Present at user groups** — Show the tool at a local or virtual Power
  Platform user group meetup.
- **Mention in documentation** — If you write guides or tutorials, link to
  the open-source tools you used.
- **Recommend to colleagues** — If someone asks "How do you do X?", point
  them to the open-source solution.

## Summary

| Action | Effort | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Star a repository | 1 second | Boosts visibility and trust |
| Report a bug | 10 minutes | Saves maintainers hours |
| Join a discussion | 5 minutes | Builds community |
| Watch for releases | 1 second | Stay informed |
| Sponsor a maintainer | Monthly | Sustains the ecosystem |
| Advocate publicly | 15 minutes | Attracts users and contributors |

!!! success "Every action counts"
    You do not need to do all of these. Even a single star, a well-written
    bug report, or a mention in a blog post makes a meaningful difference
    to the people who build the tools you depend on.
