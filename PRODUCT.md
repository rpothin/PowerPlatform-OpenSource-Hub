# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three distinct personas, all members of the Power Platform developer and maker community:

- **Maintainers** — own or are core contributors to an open-source Power Platform project. They want visibility, community growth, and ecosystem momentum tracking.
- **Contributors** — occasionally contribute to open-source projects. They need a frictionless way to find projects actively seeking help (good-first-issue, help-wanted).
- **Users** — Power Platform makers and developers looking for community-built tools, templates, and solutions to accelerate their own work.

All three are already familiar with Microsoft tooling and GitHub conventions. They arrive with intent, not curiosity; they're looking for something specific.

## Product Purpose

A community-driven discovery hub for open-source Power Platform and Copilot Studio projects. The hub continuously tracks hundreds of GitHub repositories, surfaces contribution opportunities, and helps maintainers grow their communities. It is the bridge between the Power Platform ecosystem and the open-source world.

Success looks like: more people discovering projects, more issues getting picked up, and more maintainers feeling their work is visible.

## Brand Personality

Better-together, trustworthy, engaging.

The hub serves the community — it does not sell to it. It earns trust by being accurate, useful, and transparent. It engages by surfacing the right project to the right person at the right moment. It embodies "better together" by making the collective output of the community visible and navigable.

## Anti-references

- Marketing/sales aesthetics: no hero metrics designed to impress, no conversion funnels, no manufactured urgency. This is not a product page; it is a community utility.
- Generic SaaS landing pages with gradient stats, hero-metric templates, or pitch-deck layouts.
- Anything that feels like it's asking the user to buy something.

## Design Principles

1. **Bridge the familiar** — blend Microsoft Fluent design patterns with GitHub's utility vocabulary. Power Platform community members know both worlds; the hub should feel like home in both.
2. **Community-first, not conversion-first** — every element serves discovery and contribution, not funnel mechanics. Remove anything that optimizes for impressions over usefulness.
3. **Trust through clarity** — data, actions, and labels must be self-evident. No dark patterns, no manufactured urgency, no mystery counts.
4. **Better together** — surface connections: between projects, contributors, maintainers, and ideas. The collective is the product.
5. **Function earns the aesthetic** — when in doubt, clarity and usefulness beat decoration. Visual choices justify themselves by making the content easier to navigate or understand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as a baseline — reasonable inclusivity without excessive overhead. Prioritize: sufficient color contrast (≥4.5:1 body text), keyboard navigability for search and filters, and `prefers-reduced-motion` support for any animations. The gallery's search and filter flows are the highest-risk a11y surfaces; those get the most attention.
